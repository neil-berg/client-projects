About

Meredith Lackey is a filmmaker based in Los Angeles. She holds a BA in Philosophy and Media from Hampshire College, and an MFA in Moving Image from the University of Illinois Chicago. Her work has screened internationally at festivals including Sundance, NYFF, FIDMarseille, and Edinburgh. She is the recipient of the UFVA Carole Fielding Grant and is a Princess Grace Foundation Honorarium winner.

Work

Cablestreet

2019 / 23’ / 4K / 5.1

A cable system designed by controversial Chinese company Huawei Technologies enables communication between an expert and a machine. Time succumbs to space in a New Cold War played out in materials.

2019 Sundance Film Festival - World Premier

Operation Car Wash /
Operação Lava Jato

2019 / Super 16mm / stereo

Car wash workers wash cars as The National Confederation of Agricultural Workers protests the removal of Brazilian President Dilma Rousseff. Open-carry advocates clash with ANTIFA as the Republican National Committee nominates Donald Trump. Operation Car Wash is a verité portrait of populism as seen from the public square.

Produced with support from the Sacatar Foundation
